About Me

My name is Franz Hoerwick, and I usually go by the nickname "fhoerwick" online. I am a twenty year old information technology fan from the north-central USA. The primary factor that cultivated my interest in computers and the internet was the freedom they gave me to explore and create. I still greatly appreciate both those freedoms and the the ability to communicate with people from many different backgrounds, nationalities, and walks of life.

My main interests in the vast information technology field include the following:

  • The Internet and Legislation/Civil Rights
  • Free and Open Source Software Community Models
  • Free and Open Source Software Development Models
  • Non-Commercial Usage of the Internet
  • Alternative Internet-Based Business Models

About this Site

In addition to the topics listed above, my use of the Debian, Fedora, and Slackware Linux distributions will definitely lead to comments about my experiences or scripts related to those distros finding their way here.

Secure communication methods are something I value, and some are methods I wish to make monologues about. This will likely include guidelines and instructional videos.

While this site does not have interaction features like comments, I enourage both responses and constructive criticism. Feel free to contact me by email or on Freenode's IRC network. I use the nick "fhoerwick" and can often be found in the ##freebsd, ##linux, ##slackware, and #debian channels.

If your correspondence is at all sensitive, I strongly suggest encrypting it using my public PGP key. For private messages on IRC, you can enter the command
/query fhoerwick
to open an interface which you can use to send a message others in the channels I am in cannot see. In addition, I have a functional OTR messaging plugin, which you are also free to use.
